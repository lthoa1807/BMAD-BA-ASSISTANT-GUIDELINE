# Step 3: Discovery Brief

## Goal

Produce a concise business discovery brief that can feed fit-gap analysis and requirements definition.

## Instructions

Use the prior step records as source inputs. If a step record is missing, ask for the minimum missing context or recreate a concise record before writing the brief.

Write the brief with these sections:

- Executive summary
- Scope and business outcomes
- Stakeholder map
- Process areas and current-state summary
- Systems, data, integrations, reports, and controls
- Pain points and quantified impacts where known
- Design or delivery implications
- Assumptions, risks, decisions, and open questions
- Recommended next step

Preserve trace IDs from the step records so readers can link the brief back to original findings, decisions, risks, and questions.

## Step Documentation

Create or update `{ba_artifacts}/discovery/step-03-brief-notes.md` with:

- Workflow and step name
- Inputs reviewed, including prior discovery step records
- Questions asked
- User responses and evidence
- Briefing findings with `DISC-###` IDs
- Decisions with `DEC-###` IDs
- Assumptions
- Risks with `RISK-###` IDs
- Open questions with `Q-###` IDs
- Output fragment
- Final artifact path

Update `{ba_artifacts}/ba-worklog-index.md` with workflow completion status, artifact path, carried-forward risks, open questions, and recommended next workflow.

Flag any area that needs a workshop before fit-gap analysis.

## Completion

Save the brief under `{ba_artifacts}/discovery/` and report the path to the user.
